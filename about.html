Academy for Credit Education

Provides streamlined solutions for credit counselors and debt negotiators seeking required certification under the Uniform Debt-Management Services Act. Here you can access exam study materials, test coordination and continuing education opportunities.

Our Mission

Is to increase professionalism, standards and positive recognition in the credit counseling and debt settlement industries. We believe that credit counselors and debt settlement specialists provide an essential service to consumers in debt and that education is key for enhanced on-the-job effectiveness, employee satisfaction and client retention.

Our Story

In 2002, ACE (formerly NIFCE) commissioned the first ever job/needs analysis of credit counseling with Dr. Richard Griffith of the Center for Professional Development at Florida Tech as Senior Research Consultant.

Needs Analysis NIFCE

Curriculum development was conducted through the Center for Financial Literacy, a 501 (c) (3) non profit agency, and overseen by training specialist John Aiello, M.S. Education. Research and testing contributors included Industrial/Organizational Psychology professionals Amanda Evans, Ph.D, Matthew Monnett, Ph.D,Kelly Bradbury, M.S. and Abhishek Gujar, M.S.

The result was the first edition of Keys to Success Credit Counselor Certification Program. A professional association was then founded to oversee ethics, testing and continuing education. Keys to Success is now in its fifth edition and the Association of Debt Counselors (see affiliates) oversees certification.

The Academy for Credit Education has taken Keys to Success into online accessibility with web-based learning and trainer tools. ACE also provides links to Kindle/Smartphone ebooks as well as hard copy study guides.

Our Standards

  1. Credit counselors as well as other financial-based counselors must be certified.
  2. Certification is earned through an independent educational organization.
  3. Curriculum is research-based and industry-specific.
  4. Exams are written in accordance with The Standards for Educational and Psychological Testing.
  5. Testing is administered by qualified, independent proctoring services.
  6. Certification is awarded to candidates who score 70% or higher on the exam.
  7. Certification must be renewed every two years by completing 16 hours of continuing education.
  8. All continuing education completed for the purposes of certification renewal must be approval by the Association of Debt Counselors Advisory Board.
