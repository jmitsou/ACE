Frequently Asked Questions
  • What is ACE?

    ACE stands for the Academy for Credit Education. Formerly the National Institute for Financial Counseling Education, ACE is a professional training and credentialing agency designed for credit counselors, debt negotiators and other financial-based counseling professionals seeking approved certification and continuing education courses. ACE was established to provide online accessibility to the nationally recognized Keys to Success Exam Study Course.

  • What is the Association of Debt Counselors?

    The Association of Debt Counselors (ADC) is a professional organization that oversees the educational and testing procedures of its members. ADC awards certificates to successful certification candidates.

  • Another affiliate is the Center for Financial Literacy. Who are they?

    The Center for Financial Literacy (CFL) is a 501 (c)(3) responsible for research and development of curriculum used for certifiying credit counselors and other financial-based counseling professionals. This organization initiated the first needs analysis of the credit counseling and debt settlements industries.

  • Are your programs recognized and approved by governing bodies?

    Yes, they are recognized by the US Trustees Office of the Department of Justice which oversees the implementation of consumer legislation. We are also recognized by individual states requiring the certification of credit counselors and debt settlement specialists. Finally, Keys to Success is a recognized certification program throughout the debt management and debt settlement industries.

  • How long have you been in operation?

    Keys to Success Certification Program was established in 2000 and was offered initially through the National Institute for Financial Counseling Education. ACE has been established more recently to offer the certification program as an online course.

  • How many counselors have been certified through Keys to Success?

    Keys to Success has certified thousands of credit counselors, debt settlement specialists and other financial-based counseling professionals nationwide.

  • Who are some of your past clients?

    We have worked with large organizations such as CareOne, National Foundation for Debt Management and Lighthouse Credit Foundation. We have also provided services for small community-based agencies as well as individuals operating independently. Many of our clients represent faith-based counseling organizations such as Family Life Credit Services and Catholic Charities Credit Counseling.

  • Can you tell me about your programs?

    Our counselor certification programs cover:

    1. - essential counseling techniques
    2. - financial problem solving
    3. - consumer credit
    4. - consumer protection legislation
    5. - And the sources and solutions of delinquent debt

    Our curriculum is holistic in that it emphasizes not only the financial side of debt-related issues but also the emotional/cognitive aspects related to destructive money behaviors.
